# Stakeholder Framing
*[Date] — [Audience label]*

## The audience
[Who this is written for. Their role, their priorities, what they're likely skeptical about, what they need to feel confident saying yes.]

## What I'm bringing to them
[The proposal, decision, or update — in one sentence, in their language. Not "here's what I built" but "here's why you should care."]

## Why it matters to them specifically
[Connect the work to what they own or are accountable for. Translate from your frame to theirs.]

## What I'm asking for
[The specific yes — a decision, access, resource, endorsement, or simply awareness. Be precise. Vague asks get vague responses.]

## Potential concerns and how to address them
[What they'll likely push back on. How to respond in a way that's honest and moves the conversation forward.]

## The one thing they should remember
[If they forget everything else, what's the single point that needs to stick.]
